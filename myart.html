Animations

Being able to capture a concept I came up with in animation is true beauty to me. Growing up I always used lose myself in while watching animation movies from Disney, Pixar, Dreamworks and so many more. These are the movies that taught me to dream big and showed me that if you work really hard and have the heart for it, you will create wonderful things. On this page you'll see a couple animations I worked on!

I did this animation of a plant for a school assignment! It was for an elective called "The art of storytelling". For our first assignment, we had to tell a story about a plant that had a problem. I thought this would be the perfect moment to challenge myself to animate something and get super creative with how i was gonna do this. I wanted to finish this animation in one day and and make something that was short but sweet. I came up with this idea of a plant that was alone in the rain and simpely wanted some company. All the sudden this other charater (bunny) Would walk by looking for shelter, and finding that shelter with this plant.

To make this animation in 1 day I picked a very simple art style. Something i knew I could draw fast and consistent. I already worked on a simular animatic for another school assignment some time before making this one (Click to see that animation ). So i used that storyboard instead of having to make one from scratch. Doing this allowed me to immediately start animating! It took me 16 hours total to make this animation, and yes, I did it all in 1 day :)

This animation took me 1 day aswell. I want to make a trailer like animation. I did this to practice my understanding in cinematography Most of the fragments you see are high quality drawings I did. I want to animate only one thing in this animation, which was the main character. I had never animated humans, so I wanted to make the animation as smooth as I could. The anime "Violet Evergarden" was my big inspiration for this animations. You can also see a bit of the style of that anime coming back in the design of the character. And I of course used a track of the soundtrack called "A Dolls Beginning". This is very important, because I also wanted to visualize the images/animation I imagined while listening to this track. The character you see in this animation are members from one of my favourite bands (Palaye Royale). I thought it would make the proces more fun, if I make art about something I'm interested in!

This was my first animation ever made. I called it 'The Bee'. I wanted to see what I could make without zero experience in animated. With this animation I wanted to practice the principles of animation. With the walking cycle I wanted to practice timing and spacing, to make it so that the walking looked smooth. I always like it when characters have over exaggerated expressions in cartoons. I want to also try this when the character stops and sees the bee it's neck grows longer, because it first passes the bee by like it's nothing intresting than realizes he just saw this cute bee sleeping in a flower. I tried to use squash and stretch to achieve that. I wanted the character's movements to synchronize with the music, reminiscent of the style seen in old Disney movies. Interestingly, I achieved this purely by feeling.

Concept Art

Concept art serves as the foundation for storytelling, enabling me to visualize and design characters, environments, and narratives from scratch and make them come alive. I love the process of ideation and iteration, where every stroke of the pen or brush holds the potential to shape an entire world. Concept art is not just about creating pretty pictures; it's about capturing the essence of a story, conveying emotions, and sparking imagination. For me, there's nothing more fulfilling than seeing an idea evolve from a simple sketch to a fully realized work of art.



The concept of Elias von Helach, a boss design I did for an unfinished game, emerged through a series of iterations and diverse inspirations. Drawing from the dark art style of Bloodborne, I aimed to capture the eerie and foreboding atmosphere synonymous with the game. Additionally, the tragic transformation of Nina Tucker in Fullmetal Alchemist, where her innocence is twisted into a monstrous form by her father, served as inspiration for Elias's character. I also found inspiration in the character Jacob Marley from the movie Christmas Carol 2009, particularly the chilling sound of his chains. The haunting resonance of Marley's chains in the film used is terrifying to me, and I wanted a similar sense of dread through Elias's design, incorporating symbolic elements of chains to enhance his ominous presence. Through the fusion of these influences, Elias von Helach emerged as a character that embodies both the haunting ambience from Bloodborne, the tragic transformation depicted in Fullmetal Alchemist, and the haunting presence of Jacob Marley."



When tasked with creating an scene for a VR game for the company I work at, Sync Vr, I came up with creating an Underwater Cave scene. I wanted to design an environment that would provide a relaxing and immersive experience for the player. Understanding the importance of spatial design in VR, I focused on creating an environment with nice spacing to enhance the sense of depth and immersion. Closed-off spaces in VR can often feel claustrophobic, so I aimed to counteract this by designing the cave with multiple openings, allowing the player to feel a sense of openness and freedom within the environment. Additionally, I was conscious of the common association of caves with darkness and uncertainty, which can evoke feelings of fear or unease. To avoid this, I ensured that the cave was well-lit and included ample natural lighting sources to create a warm and inviting atmosphere. By incorporating these elements, I aimed to create a tranquil and welcoming underwater cave scene that would provide players with a serene and enjoyable experience.
